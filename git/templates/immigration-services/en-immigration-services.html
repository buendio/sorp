How can my family and I move to the UAE?

You first need to apply for a permanent resident visa, followed by your family visa’s, which are issued on the receipt of offical documents, such as birth and marriage certificates. There are several routes to consider:

1. Register an onshore company
Get a permanent residency when you own a local company or business in a UAE Free Trade Zone.
2. Become an investor in business projects
Receive residency when you make an investment and/or become a partner in an existing business project company
3. Make investments in real estate
Buy your own slice of real estate in the UAE and you can apply for your permanent residency.

Why business immigration is unique in the UAE

Residency terms
A permanent residency is issued up to a standard period of three years with the possibility of extending this to a further five and/or ten-year visa.
No - restriction residencies
There are no restrictions for permanent residence in the UAE. Re-entry is allowed in the country once every 180 days that promotes greater freedom.
Tax affiliation
If a number of conditions are met, you will qualify for a tax domicile certificate confirming the status and tax affiliation in the United Arib Emirates.
Zero taxes
Being a resident of the UAE means that you pay zero corporate and zero personal income taxes. This applies to businesss set up in the Free Trade Zone.
Access to financial services
All residents have the opportunity to open bank accounts in the UAE, make investments and have access to a credit facility.
Protection of information
The UAE legislation protects and limits the automatic exchange of tax data on residential accounts and their personal properties.
