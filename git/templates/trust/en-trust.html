A trust is a proven by decades and recognized tool that ensures the confidentiality and security of owner’s property rights. Classical jurisdictions of Europe and the United Kingdom, where trust legislation originated and has been existing over several centuries, lose their relevance and no longer guarantee the anonymity of their owners due to stricter disclosure requirements and the availability of open registers. In this regard, the Arab trust in the United Arab Emirates is becoming a highly reliable and tool that guarantees preserving anonymity

How does the Arab trust work?

What is the Arab Trust

The Arab trust is a trust relationship in the classical sense, but working according to the UAE legislation based on the principles of Sharia law.

Legally, an Arab trust is created by signing a pool of contracts and agreements based on the Commercial Law of the United Arab Emirates and approved in the Dubai Court.
Who is part of the Arab Trust
Get a consultation
As a European trust, Arab Trust has an owner, trustee and beneficiary.

The owner is the nominal shareholder; the beneficiary is the investor, who receives the benefits from the trust.

The manager of business - a nominal director, who performs all instructions of the final beneficiary, he plays the role of trustee.

The UAE supports all international information disclosure standards. This applies only to non-residents of the UAE.

All participants of trusts are residents (investors can receive this status), and protection against automatic information exchange is guaranteed.

The courts of the United Arab Emirates, where the trust relations are established, are not involved in the automatic exchange of information.

The state policy of the UAE is entirely aimed at creating favorable conditions and equal rights for foreign investors because of ideal conditions for doing business and the absence of corporate taxes (with the exception of 5% VAT on a number of operations within the domestic market).

Taxation in the UAE is zero for financial and investment transactions, as well as all international activities.

In accordance with the existing legal framework the UAE identifies the following main goals of the Arab trust
Confidentiality
The owners of assets are provided with absolute anonymity at the state level as information is concealed from third parties
Freedom of interdependent operations
The ability to carry out tax-free movement of funds and assets due to the anonymity of the owners of the trust
Protection of investors' assets
Trust confidentiality allows you to protect the business and assets of its owners with the ability to implement effective management
Guaranteed and tax-free inheritance
The possibility of registering the inheritance of assets to certain heirs by the owners of persons with tax benefits
Collective Investment
Accumulation of funds in a single Fund managed by a trust for the purpose of guaranteed investment
Concealment of ownership of real estate
By registering residential and commercial properties for a trust structure, you will retain ownership without the need to declare them

The Arab types of trusts

Choose the type of Arab trust that suits Your goals and objectives
Fixed trust or a trust of joint ownership
The beneficiary of the trust is not recorded as the ultimate owner of any of the founding structures of the trust. This makes his position more secure from third party claims.

The distribution of assets is determined by the ultimate beneficiary on the basis of decisions made by him, the ownership right is formalized by agreement of the parties and by pledging shares. The ultimate owner and beneficiary must be closed with UAE tax resident status.
